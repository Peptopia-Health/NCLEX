Acid-Base Balance & Ventilators

Lec 1

Rule of the B's

If pH and bicarb go the same direction → Metabolic. If they go different directions → Respiratory.

ValueNormal RangeMemory Tip
pH7.35 – 7.45Below 7.35 = acidosis · Above 7.45 = alkalosis
HCO₃ (bicarb)22 – 262+2+2 = 6 (remember 22–26)
CO₂35 – 45Inverse relationship with pH (CO₂ up → pH down)
As the pH goes, so goes my patient — EXCEPT potassium (inverse)!

Compensation Rules

  • Uncompensated: pH abnormal; only ONE other value off
  • Partially compensated: pH abnormal; BOTH CO₂ and HCO₃ off (body trying to fix it)
  • Fully compensated: pH NORMAL; both CO₂ and HCO₃ off (body succeeded)

↑ Alkalosis (pH Up)

  • Irritability, hyperreflexia (3+/4+)
  • Tachypnea, tachycardia
  • Borborygmi (↑ bowel sounds)
  • Seizure risk, aspiration risk
  • Tingling, numbness, muscle cramps

↓ Acidosis (pH Down)

  • Hyporeflexia (1+/2+), lethargy
  • Bradycardia, hypotension
  • Paralytic ileus (↓ bowel sounds)
  • Coma, respiratory arrest
  • Warm, flushed skin (vasodilation)
MAC Kussmaul — M: Metabolic, AC: Acidosis → deep labored breathing (DKA, renal failure, salicylate OD)

Common Clinical Causes

ImbalanceCommon Causes
Resp. AcidosisCOPD, oversedation, pneumonia, airway obstruction
Resp. AlkalosisHyperventilation, anxiety, pain, fever, PE
Met. AcidosisDKA, renal failure, shock, diarrhea, salicylate OD
Met. AlkalosisProlonged vomiting, NG suction, excess antacids, diuretics

Quick Decision Tree

  1. Is it lung? → Respiratory
  2. Over-ventilating → alkalosis · Under-ventilating → acidosis
  3. Prolonged gastric vomiting/suctioning → always metabolic alkalosis
  4. Everything else that isn't lung → metabolic acidosis
If you don't know the answer, choose metabolic acidosis — it's the most common imbalance!

Ventilator Alarms

AlarmCauseResultAction
High Pressure↑ Resistance (obstruction)Resp. alkalosisKink → water → mucus → suction (in order)
Low Pressure↓ Resistance (disconnection)Resp. acidosisReconnect tubing; if O₂ tube on floor → bag patient & call RT
If the ventilator malfunctions → disconnect and manually ventilate with an Ambu bag. NEVER leave a patient without ventilation!

Maslow's Hierarchy (Highest → Lowest)

Physiological → Safety → Comfort → Psychological → Social → Spiritual

  • Physiological needs ALWAYS come first (airway, breathing, circulation)
  • On the NCLEX: if stuck between two answers, pick the more basic need

Alcoholism & Substance Abuse

Lec 2

Key Concepts

ProblemDefinitionTreatment
#1 DenialRefusal to accept realityConfront it directly
#2 DependencyAbuser gets others to enableSet limits & enforce; learn to say NO
ManipulationGetting others to do harmful actsSet limits; no harm = dependent, harmful = manipulation
Abuse = CONFRONT · Loss & Grief (Denial→Anger→Bargaining→Depression→Acceptance) = SUPPORT

Wernicke-Korsakoff Syndrome (WKS)

  • Caused by chronic thiamine (B1) deficiency from prolonged alcohol abuse
  • Wernicke's (acute): confusion, ataxia, ophthalmoplegia — reversible if treated
  • Korsakoff's (chronic): amnesia with confabulation (making up stories; they believe it)
  • Treatment: Redirect the patient (do NOT confront confabulation)
  • Preventable & arrestable with vitamin B1, but ~70% irreversible once Korsakoff's develops

Antabuse (Disulfiram)

Aversion therapy — takes 2 weeks to enter and leave the system.

Avoid ALL alcohol sources: mouthwash, aftershave, perfume, insect repellant, elixirs, alcohol-based hand sanitizers, uncooked icings. CAN have red wine vinaigrette (alcohol cooked off). Patient must give informed consent.

Uppers vs. Downers

UppersDowners
ExamplesCaffeine, cocaine, PCP/LSD, meth, AdderallHeroin, alcohol, marijuana, barbiturates, opioids
Signs↑ Euphoria, tachycardia, diarrhea, reflexes 3+/4+, dilated pupils↓ Lethargy, resp depression, bradycardia, reflexes 1+/2+, pinpoint pupils
OverdoseToo much UP → pick ↑ signs (seizures, hyperthermia)Too much DOWN → pick ↓ signs (resp arrest, coma)
WithdrawalToo little UP → pick ↓ signs (depression, crash)Too little DOWN → pick ↑ signs (tremors, seizures)
Upper OD looks like Downer withdrawal · Downer OD looks like Upper withdrawal — they're opposites!

Highest Priority Emergencies

  • Respiratory depression/arrest: Downer OD or Upper withdrawal
  • Seizure: Upper OD or Downer withdrawal
  • Respiratory depression > seizure in priority (airway always first)

Specific Substance Notes

  • PCP (Angel Dust): Most dangerous — unpredictable violence. Quiet room, minimal stimulation. Do NOT talk down
  • LSD: Hallucinations. CAN talk down. Stay with patient
  • Cocaine: Vasoconstriction → MI risk even in young people
  • Opioid OD antidote: Narcan (Naloxone)

Newborn Drug Addiction

At birth → assume intoxication. After 24 hours → assume withdrawal.

  • Withdrawal signs: high-pitched cry, tremors, irritability, poor feeding, sneezing
  • Swaddle tightly, decrease stimulation, small frequent feedings

Aminoglycosides, CCBs & Cardiac Arrhythmias

Lec 3

Aminoglycosides — "A Mean Old Mycin"

Powerful antibiotics (BIG GUNS) for serious gram-negative infections including TB.

NOT mean old mycins: erythromycin, azithromycin, clarithromycin — if it has "thro," throw it off the list!
  • Ototoxic: monitor hearing, tinnitus, vertigo (cranial nerve VIII)
  • Nephrotoxic: monitor creatinine (best kidney function indicator)
  • Admin Q8H · Route: IM or IV only (NOT absorbed PO)
  • Oral mycins → used for hepatic coma (kills GI bacteria → lowers ammonia)
  • Common: Gentamicin, Tobramycin, Amikacin, Streptomycin, Neomycin

Trough & Peak Levels

TimingRule
Trough30 min BEFORE next dose (any route)
Peak (IV)15–30 min after infusion ends
Peak (IM)30–60 min after injection
Trough = enough drug? · Peak = too much drug (toxicity)?

Calcium Channel Blockers — "Valium for the Heart"

  • Negative inotropic, chronotropic, dromotropic = calms the heart
  • Treats: hypertension, angina, atrial arrhythmias, SVT
  • Names: end in -dipine + Cardizem + Verapamil
  • Side effects: headache, hypotension, dizziness, constipation
  • No grapefruit juice (increases drug levels)
Hold CCB if systolic BP < 100!

You can SHOCK a wiggle (V-Fib) but CANNOT shock a flatline (Asystole)!

Chest Tubes

Purpose: re-establish negative pressure in the pleural space.

  • Apical (high, 2nd intercostal) = air (pneumothorax) — air rises
  • Basilar (low, 8th–9th intercostal) = blood (hemothorax) — fluid sinks
  • Keep drainage system BELOW chest level; monitor for >100 mL/hr drainage
Water seal breaks → FIRST: clamp · BEST: submerge under sterile water · Tube pulled out → FIRST: gloved hand · BEST: vaseline gauze

Chest Tube Bubbling

Water sealContinuous❌ BAD — air leak!
Suction controlIntermittent❌ BAD — suction too low
Suction controlContinuous✅ GOOD — document
Do NOT clamp longer than 15 seconds without a doctor's order!

Congenital Heart Defects · Infection Precautions · Crutches

Lec 3–4

Congenital Heart Defects — TRouBLe

TROUBLE (Right → Left, Cyanotic)

  • All start with "T": Tetralogy of Fallot, Truncus arteriosus, Transposition, Tricuspid atresia, TAPVR
  • Exception: L. ventricular hypoplastic syndrome
  • Deoxygenated blood enters systemic circulation
  • Needs surgery, delayed growth, ↓ life expectancy
  • Tet spells: knee-chest position

NO TROUBLE (Left → Right, Pink)

  • VSD, PDA, PFO, ASD, pulmonic stenosis, coarctation of aorta
  • Oxygenated blood recirculates → volume overload
  • Often no surgery needed, normal growth
  • Long-term risk: CHF from ↑ pulmonary blood flow
Tetralogy of Fallot = 4 defects: VSD, Pulmonic Stenosis, Overriding Aorta, Right ventricular Hypertrophy

Transmission-Based Precautions

TypeExamplesKey PPE / Requirements
ContactC. diff, Hep A, MRSA, RSV, herpes, scabiesGloves + gown + hand washing; private room preferred
DropletMeningitis, H. flu, pertussis, influenzaSurgical mask + gloves; patient masks when leaving; 3 ft distance
AirborneMeasles, mumps, rubella, TB, varicellaPrivate room REQUIRED, N95 (TB), negative airflow
PPE Off: Gloves → Goggles → Gown → Mask · PPE On: Gown → Mask → Goggles → Gloves
C. diff: use SOAP & WATER — alcohol sanitizers do NOT kill C. diff spores!

  • Weight on HANDS, not axillae (prevents crutch palsy)

Insulin Administration

  • Store unopened in fridge; opened vial at room temp (up to 28 days)
  • Roll NPH gently (don't shake)
  • Rotate injection sites within same region; abdomen absorbs fastest
  • Do NOT inject into limb about to be exercised

Exercise = Another Shot of Insulin

  • More exercise → need less insulin
  • Less exercise → need more insulin
  • Sick days: glucose goes UP → still take insulin, sip water, stay active

Acute Complications

Hypoglycemia

  • S/S: "Drunk in shock" — staggering, slurred, cold/pale/clammy, tremors
  • Tx: 15g fast sugar → recheck 15 min → complex carb/protein
  • Unconscious → Glucagon IM or D10/D50 IV
  • Danger: permanent brain damage
  • Higher priority than DKA

DKA (Type 1 only)

  • #1 cause: acute viral URI in last 2 weeks
  • S/S: Dehydration, Kussmaul, high K⁺, Acidosis, Acetone breath
  • Tx: HYDRATE (200mL/hr NS), Regular insulin IV drip
  • D5 OK when glucose <250; NOT D10/D50
  • Watch K⁺: initially high, drops fast with insulin
HHNK (Type 2) = same treatment (hydrate!) · Higher mortality but DKA has higher NCLEX priority.

Electrolytes & Endocrine

Lec 7

Electrolyte Rules

Kalemia (K⁺) = SAME as prefix (except HR & urine) · Calcemia (Ca²⁺) = OPPOSITE of prefix · Magnesemia (Mg²⁺) = OPPOSITE of prefix
SystemHYPER-kalemiaHYPO-kalemia
BrainIrritable, restlessLethargic, confused
LungsTachypneaBradypnea
HeartBradycardia ★Tachycardia ★
UrineOliguria ★Polyuria ★
BowelDiarrheaConstipation/ileus
MusclesSpasticityFlaccidity
Reflexes3+/4+1+/2+

★ = opposite of prefix (exception!)

Calcium & Magnesium — Opposite Pattern

  • HYPOcalcemia/HYPOmag → HYPER signs (spasms, hyperreflexia, seizures, tetany)
  • HYPERcalcemia/HYPERmag → HYPO signs (lethargy, flaccidity, cardiac arrest)
Chvostek sign (tap cheek → face spasm) & Trousseau sign (BP cuff → hand spasm) = HYPOcalcemia or HYPOmagnesemia

Sodium Quick Rules

  • HYPERnatremia = Dehydration (DKA, DI, HHNK)
  • HYPOnatremia = Fluid overload (SIADH, CHF)
  • Numbness & tingling = earliest sign of ANY electrolyte disorder
  • Muscle weakness = universal sign

Treating High Potassium

  1. D5W + Regular Insulin (fast/temporary — drives K⁺ into cells) "enters early"
  2. Kayexalate (slow/permanent — swaps K⁺ for Na⁺) "K exits late"
  3. Give BOTH → switching from life-threatening (hyperK) to non-life-threatening (hyperNa) → then hydrate!
  4. Calcium gluconate IV → protects heart (does NOT lower K⁺)
Hyperkalemia → peaked T waves → widened QRS → sine wave → cardiac arrest. Life-threatening!

Endocrine Overview

HYPERthyroidism (Graves)

  • = HYPERmetabolism (everything speeds up)
  • Weight loss, ↑ pulse/BP, irritable, heat intolerance, exophthalmos, tremors
  • Tx: radioactive iodine (isolate 24hrs, flush 3×), PTU (monitor WBCs), thyroidectomy

HYPOthyroidism (Myxedema)

  • = HYPOmetabolism (everything slows down)
  • Obese, cold intolerance, ↓ pulse/BP, constipation, fatigue
  • Tx: Synthroid (levothyroxine) — lifelong
  • Do NOT sedate; NEVER hold thyroid pills

Post-Thyroidectomy Nursing

  • Tracheostomy tray at bedside (laryngeal nerve damage risk)
  • Check behind neck for bleeding (blood pools posteriorly)
  • Watch for hypocalcemia (parathyroid damage) → Chvostek/Trousseau
  • Support head/neck; semi-Fowler's position

Thyroid Storm — Medical Emergency!

Temps >105°F, extreme HTN, severe tachycardia (180–200), delirium. FIRST: ice packs · BEST: cooling blanket + O₂ 10L mask. Do NOT use Tylenol or aspirin!

Addison's vs. Cushing's

Addison's (UNDERsecretion)Cushing's (OVERsecretion)
S/SHyperpigmented, ↓ glucose/BP/Na⁺, ↑ K⁺, weight lossMoon face, buffalo hump, hirsutism, ↑ glucose/BP/Na⁺, ↓ K⁺, immunosuppressed
Treatment"ADD a SONE" (glucocorticoids)Bilateral adrenalectomy / taper steroids
CrisisAddisonian crisis → shock; IV hydrocortisone STATInfection risk (immunosuppressed)
NEVER abruptly stop steroids (causes Addisonian crisis). Stress-dose steroids before surgery.

Prioritization, Delegation & Staff Management

Lec 11

4 Rules for Prioritization

  1. Acute beats chronic (appendicitis > stable COPD)
  2. Fresh post-op (<12 hrs) beats medical or other surgical
  3. Unstable ALWAYS beats stable
  4. More vital organ = higher priority (tie-breaker only: Brain → Lung → Heart → Liver → Kidney → Pancreas)
Focus on the MODIFYING PHRASE, not the diagnosis! Do NOT default to ABCs.

Unstable vs. Stable Indicators

🔴 Unstable (RN must assess)

  • Acute illness, post-op <12 hrs
  • General anesthesia (first 12 hrs)
  • C/D-level lab abnormalities
  • Newly admitted/diagnosed
  • Changed/changing assessments
  • Unexpected S/S
  • Altered LOC

🟢 Stable (Can delegate to LPN)

Test-Taking Strategies & Guessing Rules

Bonus

Psych Test Principles

  • Examine your OWN feelings (prevent countertransference)
  • Establish trust; don't give advice, gifts, or guarantees
  • Best answer = the one that keeps the patient talking
  • Be concrete — psych patients take things literally
  • Empathy: ignore what is SAID, go with what is FELT
  • Therapeutic: open-ended questions, reflection, restating, clarifying
  • Non-therapeutic: "why" questions, false reassurance, changing subject

General Guessing Strategies

  • Nutrition: When in doubt, pick chicken (not fried); then fish (not shellfish)
  • Pharm: Know drug's action → pick side effect in same body system
  • Unknown PO drug? → pick a GI side effect (50/50)
  • OB: Always check fetal heart rate first
  • Med-Surg: First ASSESS = LOC; first DO = establish airway
  • Peds: When in doubt → call it normal, pick older age, easier task
  • Safety: If in doubt about positioning → side-lying (prevents aspiration)

Logic Rules

  • Rule out absolutes (always, never, all, none — usually wrong)
  • If 2 answers say the same thing → neither is right
  • If 2 answers are opposite → one is probably right
  • Umbrella strategy: pick the answer that covers all others
  • Worst consequences game: "If I didn't do this…" → pick worst outcome
  • Sesame Street rule: 3 belong together, 1 different → different one is correct
  • "Which would the nurse do FIRST" → almost always assessment (unless dying)
