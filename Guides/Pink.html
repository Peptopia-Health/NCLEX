Acid-Base Balance & Ventilators

Lec 1

Rule of the B's

If pH and bicarb go the same direction → Metabolic. If they go different directions → Respiratory.

ValueNormal RangeMemory Tip
pH7.35 – 7.45Below = acidosis · Above = alkalosis
HCO₃ (bicarb)22 – 262+2+2 = 6
CO₂35 – 45Inverse relationship with pH
As the pH goes, so goes my patient — EXCEPT potassium (inverse)!

↑ Alkalosis (pH Up)

  • Irritability, hyperreflexia (3+/4+)
  • Tachypnea, tachycardia
  • Borborygmi (↑ bowel sounds)
  • Seizure risk, aspiration risk

↓ Acidosis (pH Down)

  • Hyporeflexia (1+/2+)
  • Bradycardia, lethargy
  • Paralytic ileus (↓ bowel sounds)
  • Coma, respiratory arrest
MAC Kussmaul — M: Metabolic, AC: Acidosis → deep labored breathing (DKA, renal failure)

Quick Decision Tree

  1. Is it lung? → Respiratory
  2. Over-ventilating → alkalosis · Under-ventilating → acidosis
  3. Prolonged gastric vomiting/suctioning → always metabolic alkalosis
  4. Everything else that isn't lung → metabolic acidosis
If you don't know the answer, choose metabolic acidosis.

Ventilator Alarms

AlarmCauseResultAction
High Pressure↑ Resistance to airflowResp. alkalosisKink → water → mucus → suction (in order)
Low Pressure↓ Resistance (disconnection)Resp. acidosisReconnect tubing; if O₂ tube on floor → bag & call RT

Maslow's Hierarchy (Highest → Lowest)

Physiological → Safety → Comfort → Psychological → Social → Spiritual

Alcoholism & Substance Abuse

Lec 2

Key Concepts

ProblemDefinitionTreatment

#2 DependencyAbuser gets others to enableSet limits & enforce; learn to say NO
ManipulationGetting others to do harmful actsSet limits; no harm = dependent, harmful = manipulation
Abuse = CONFRONT · Loss & Grief (Denial→Anger→Bargaining→Depression→Acceptance) = SUPPORT

Wernicke-Korsakoff Syndrome (WKS)

  • Caused by thiamine (B1) deficiency
  • Primary symptom: amnesia with confabulation (making up stories; they believe it)
  • Treatment: Redirect the patient
  • Preventable & arrestable with vitamin B1, but ~70% irreversible

Antabuse (Disulfiram)

Aversion therapy — takes 2 weeks to enter and leave the system.

Avoid: mouthwash, aftershave, perfume, insect repellant, elixirs, alcohol-based hand sanitizers, uncooked icings. CAN have red wine vinaigrette.

Uppers vs. Downers

UppersDowners
ExamplesCaffeine, cocaine, PCP/LSD, meth, AdderallHeroin, alcohol, marijuana
Signs↑ Euphoria, tachycardia, diarrhea, reflexes 3+/4+↓ Lethargy, resp depression, bradycardia, reflexes 1+/2+
OverdoseToo much UP → pick ↑ signsToo much DOWN → pick ↓ signs
WithdrawalToo little UP → pick ↓ signsToo little DOWN → pick ↑ signs
Upper OD looks like Downer withdrawal · Downer OD looks like Upper withdrawal

Highest Priority Emergencies

  • Respiratory depression/arrest: Downer OD or Upper withdrawal
  • Seizure: Upper OD or Downer withdrawal

Newborn Drug Addiction

At birth → assume intoxication. After 24 hours → assume withdrawal.

Aminoglycosides, CCBs & Cardiac Arrhythmias

Lec 3

Aminoglycosides — "A Mean Old Mycin"

Powerful antibiotics (the BIG GUNS) for serious, life-threatening, gram-negative infections including TB.

NOT mean old mycins: erythromycin, azithromycin, clarithromycin — if it has "thro," throw it off the list!
  • Ototoxic: monitor hearing, tinnitus, vertigo (cranial nerve #8)
  • Nephrotoxic: monitor creatinine (best indicator for kidney function)
  • Admin Q8H · Route: IM or IV only (NOT absorbed PO)
  • Oral mycins → used for hepatic coma (ammonia levels)

Trough & Peak Levels

TimingRule
Trough30 min BEFORE any route (Sub, IV, IM, SubQ, PO)
Peak (IV)15–30 min after
Peak (IM)30–60 min after
Peak (SubQ)See diabetes lecture

Calcium Channel Blockers — "Valium for the Heart"

  • Negative inotropic, chronotropic, dromotropic = calms the heart
  • Treats: hypertension, angina, atrial arrhythmias, SVT
  • Names: end in -dipine + Cardizem + Verapamil
  • Side effects: headache, hypotension
Hold CCB if systolic BP < 100!

Chest Tubes

Purpose: re-establish negative pressure in the pleural space.

  • Apical (high) = air (pneumothorax)
  • Basilar (bottom) = blood (hemothorax)
Water seal breaks → FIRST: clamp · BEST: submerge under sterile water · Tube pulled out → FIRST: gloved hand · BEST: vaseline gauze

Congenital Heart Defects · Infection Precautions · Crutches

Lec 3–4

Congenital Heart Defects — TRouBLe

TROUBLE (Right → Left, Cyanotic)

  • All start with "T": Tetralogy of Fallot, Truncus arteriosus, Transposition, Tricuspid atresia, TAPVR
  • Exception: L. ventricular hypoplastic syndrome
  • Needs surgery, delayed growth, ↓ life expectancy

NO TROUBLE (Left → Right, Pink)

  • VSD, PDA, PFO, ASD, pulmonic stenosis, coarctation of aorta
  • No surgery needed, normal growth & life expectancy
  • 24–36 hrs in hospital

Drawing up insulin: CLEAR before CLOUDY → "RN" (Regular then NPH) · Inject air into N first, then R; draw up R first, then N.

Exercise = Another Shot of Insulin

  • More exercise → need less insulin
  • Less exercise → need more insulin
  • Sick days: glucose goes UP → still take insulin, sip water, stay active

Acute Complications

Hypoglycemia

  • S/S: "Drunk in shock" — staggering, slurred speech, labile emotions, cold/pale/clammy
  • Tx: fast sugar + starch/protein (OJ & crackers)
  • Unconscious → Glucagon IM or D10/D50 IV
  • Danger: permanent brain damage

DKA (Type 1 only)

  • #1 cause: acute viral URI in last 2 weeks
  • S/S: Dehydration, Ketones/Kussmaul, high K⁺, Acidosis, Acetone breath
  • Tx: HYDRATE (200mL/hr), Regular insulin, Normal saline (D5 OK; NOT D10/D50)
HHNK (Type 2) = same treatment (hydrate!) · Higher mortality than DKA but DKA has higher priority.

Electrolytes & Endocrine

Lec 7

Electrolyte Rules

Kalemia (K⁺) = SAME as prefix (except HR & urine) · Calcemia (Ca²⁺) = OPPOSITE · Magnesemia (Mg²⁺) = OPPOSITE
SystemHYPER-kalemiaHYPO-kalemia
BrainIrritable, restlessLethargic
LungsTachypneaBradypnea
HeartBradycardia ★Tachycardia ★
UrineOliguria ★Polyuria ★
BowelDiarrheaConstipation
MusclesSpasticityFlaccidity
Reflexes3+/4+1+/2+

★ = opposite of the prefix (exception!)

Chvostek sign (cheek → face spasm) & Trousseau sign (BP cuff → hand spasm) = HYPOcalcemia

Sodium Quick Rules

  • HYPERnatremia = Dehydration (DKA, DI, HHNK)
  • HYPOnatremia = Fluid overload (SIADH)
  • Numbness & tingling (paresthesia) = earliest sign of ANY electrolyte disorder
  • Muscle weakness (paresis) = universal sign

Treating High Potassium

  1. D5W + Regular Insulin (fast/temporary — drives K⁺ into cells) "enters early"
  2. Kayexalate (slow/permanent — swaps K⁺ for Na⁺) "K exits late"
  3. Give BOTH → switching from life-threatening (hyperK) to non-life-threatening (hyperNa) → then hydrate!

Endocrine Overview

HYPERthyroidism (Graves)

  • = HYPERmetabolism
  • Weight loss, high pulse/BP, irritable, heat intolerance, exophthalmos
  • Tx: radioactive iodine (isolate 24hrs, flush 3×), PTU (immunosuppressor → monitor WBCs), thyroidectomy

HYPOthyroidism (Myxedema)

  • = HYPOmetabolism
  • Obese, cold intolerance, low pulse/BP
  • Tx: Synthroid (levothyroxine)
  • Do NOT sedate; NEVER hold thyroid pills

Thyroid Storm — Medical Emergency!

Temps >105°F, extreme HTN, severe tachycardia (180–200), psychotic delirium. FIRST: ice packs · BEST: cooling blanket + O₂ 10L mask. Do NOT use Tylenol!

Adrenocortex — Addison's vs. Cushing's

Addison's (UNDERsecretion)Cushing's (OVERsecretion)
S/SHyperpigmented, can't adapt to stress, ↓ glucose & BPMoon face, buffalo hump, hirsutism, immunosuppressed, ↑ glucose
Treatment"ADD a SONE" (glucocorticoids)Bilateral adrenalectomy

Prioritization, Delegation & Staff Management

Lec 11

4 Rules for Prioritization

  1. Acute beats chronic (appendicitis > COPD)
  2. Fresh post-op (<12 hrs) beats medical or other surgical
  3. Unstable beats stable — always!
  4. More vital organ = higher priority (tie-breaker only: Brain → Lung → Heart → Liver → Kidney → Pancreas)
Focus on the MODIFYING PHRASE, not the diagnosis! Do NOT default to ABCs.

Unstable vs. Stable Indicators

🔴 Unstable

  • Acute illness, post-op <12 hrs
  • General anesthesia (first 12 hrs)
  • C/D-level lab abnormalities
  • Newly admitted/diagnosed
  • Changed/changing assessments
  • Unexpected S/S

🟢 Stable

  • Chronic illness, post-op >12 hrs
  • Local/regional anesthesia
  • A/B-level lab abnormalities
  • Ready for discharge
  • Unchanged assessments
  • Expected/typical S/S
ALWAYS unstable regardless: hemorrhage, fever >105, hypoglycemia, pulselessness, breathlessness

LPNs CAN do more in extended care facilities (pts are stable). NEVER delegate safety responsibilities to the family!

Staff Management — Inappropriate Behavior

  1. Is it ILLEGAL? → Tell supervisor
  2. Is anyone in DANGER? → Confront & take over immediately
  3. Legal but INAPPROPRIATE? → Talk to them later
  4. NEVER ignore inappropriate behavior

Test-Taking Strategies & Guessing Rules

Bonus

Psych Test Principles

  • Examine your OWN feelings (prevent countertransference)
  • Establish trust; don't give advice, gifts, or guarantees
  • Best answer = the one that keeps the patient talking
  • Be concrete — psych patients take things literally
  • Empathy: ignore what is SAID, go with what is FELT

General Guessing Strategies

  • Nutrition: When in doubt, pick chicken (not fried); then fish (not shellfish)
  • Pharm: If you know the drug's action but not side effects → pick a side effect in the same body system
  • Unknown PO drug? → pick a GI side effect (50/50)
  • OB: Always check fetal heart rate
  • Med-Surg: First assess = LOC (not airway); first DO = establish airway
  • Peds: When in doubt, call it normal, pick the older age, pick the easier task

Logic Rules

  • Rule out absolutes (always, never)
  • If 2 answers say the same thing → neither is right
  • If 2 answers are opposite → one is probably right
  • Umbrella strategy: pick the answer that covers all others
  • Worst consequences game: "If I didn't do this…" → pick the worst outcome
  • Sesame Street rule: 3 belong together, 1 is different → the different one is correct
