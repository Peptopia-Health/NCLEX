⚗️ Acid-Base Balance & Ventilators

Lec 1

🔤 Rule of the B's

If pH and Bicarb go the same direction → Meta-B-olic. Different directions → Respiratory.

ValueNormalMemory Tip
pH7.35 – 7.45⬇️ Below = acidosis · ⬆️ Above = alkalosis
HCO₃22 – 26Bicarb — moves WITH pH in metabolic
CO₂35 – 45🔄 Inverse relationship with pH
As the pH goes, so goes my patient — EXCEPT Potassium (inverse)!

⬆️ Alkalosis (pH Up)

  • Hypokalemia, HTN, tachycardia
  • Tachypnea, seizures, irritability
  • Spastic, diarrhea, borborygmi
  • Hyperreflexia (3+/4+)

⬇️ Acidosis (pH Down)

  • Hyperkalemia, HTN, bradycardia
  • Bradypnea, lethargy, flaccid
  • Constipation, absent bowel sounds
  • Hyporeflexia (1+/2+)
MACKussmaul — only acid-base causing Kussmaul breathing = Metabolic ACidosis

🌳 Quick Decision Tree

  1. Is it lung? → Respiratory. Over-ventilating = alkalosis · Under-ventilating = acidosis
  2. Not lung? → Metabolic. Prolonged gastric vomiting/suction → alkalosis
  3. Everything else that isn't lung → metabolic acidosis (best guess)

🔔 Ventilator Alarms

AlarmCauseResultAction
High Pressure↑ ResistanceResp. alkalosis (too high)Unkink → drain water → suction mucus
Low Pressure↓ Resistance (disconnect)Resp. acidosis (too low)Reconnect tubing; tube on floor → bag & call RT

Wean = gradually decrease with the goal of getting off the ventilator altogether.

📊 Maslow's Hierarchy (Highest → Lowest)

Physiological → Safety → Comfort → Psychological → Social → Spiritual

🧠 Wernicke-Korsakoff Syndrome

  • Psychosis from Vitamin B1 (Thiamine) deficiency
  • Primary S/S: amnesia + confabulation (making up stories; they believe them) 🤥
  • Preventable & arrestable (take B1), but irreversible (kills brain cells ~70%) 🧠

💊 Antabuse / Revia (Aversion Therapy)

Onset & duration: 2 weeks to enter/leave system.

Antabuse / Revia — Avoid ALL forms of alcohol: mouthwash, cologne, aftershave, elixirs, insect repellent, hand sanitizer, vanilla extract, OTC liquid meds. 🚫🍷

🔬 Aminoglycosides & Overdose Pharmacology

Lec 2–3

🦠 Aminoglycosides — "A Mean Old Mycin"

  • Treat serious, life-threatening, gram-negative, resistant infections 🦠
  • All end in -mycin, but NOT all -mycins are aminoglycosides
  • Wannabes: Azithromycin, Clarithromycin, Erythromycin → if it has "thro," throw it off the list! 🗑️
  • Real ones: Streptomycin, Cleomycin, Tobramycin, Gentamicin, Vancomycin, Clindamycin
Mice 🐭 = Ears 👂 → Ototoxicity (CN VIII: hearing, balance, tinnitus) + Nephrotoxicity (monitor creatinine). Route: IM/IV · Q8H · "8" drawn inside the ear

💊 Oral Aminoglycosides (2 Exceptions)

  1. Hepatic encephalopathy (high ammonia) — reduce GI ammonia
  2. Pre-op bowel surgery — sterilize bowel ("Who can sterilize my bowel? Neomycin-Kanamycin") 🧹

📈 Trough & Peak Levels

RouteWhen to Draw Peak ⏱️
Sublingual5–10 min after drug dissolves
IV 💉15–30 min after medication finished
IM30–60 min after injection
SubQDepends on insulin type
PO 💊Not necessary; not tested
Trough is ALWAYS drawn 30 min BEFORE next dose regardless of route.

🫀 Cardiac: CCBs, Rhythms & Chest Tubes

Lec 3

💊 Calcium Channel Blockers — "Valium for the Heart"

Negative inotropic (weak), chronotropic (slow), dromotropic (blocks conduction).

  • Treats: HTN, angina, atrial arrhythmias (flutter & fib) 🫀
  • S/E: headache, hypotension
  • Names: "I sop zem dipine" → -dipine, Cardizem, Verapamil/Isoptin
Positive Ino/Chrono/Dromo = strong, fast, excitable (Atropine, Epinephrine). Negative = weak, slow, blocks (CCBs, beta blockers).

🫀 Beta Blockers

End in -olol. Act exactly like CCBs but also cause bronchospasm. 🚨 Never give with asthma or COPD.

💓 Cardiac Rhythms — Know These 6

RhythmKey EKG FeatureSeverity
Normal Sinus ✅Regular P-QRS-T pattern—
A-FlutterSaw-tooth P waves⚠️ Potentially lethal
A-FibChaotic P waves, irregular⚠️ Potentially lethal
V-TachWide, bizarre QRS's⚠️ Potentially lethal
V-FibChaotic, no pattern💀 LETHAL
AsystoleFlat line, no QRS💀 LETHAL

PVCs: periodic wide bizarre QRS's. Worry if: >6/min, 6 in a row, or PVC falls on T-wave. ⚠️

⚡ Arrhythmia Treatments

ArrhythmiaTreatment 💊
PVCs / V-TachLidocaine & Amiodarone
Supraventricular (ABCD)Adenocard (push FAST <8s) → Beta blockers → CCBs → Digoxin
V-Fib 💀Defibrillate! (D-FIB) ⚡
Asystole 💀Epinephrine first, then Atropine
IV push drugs: if you don't know → go SLOW.
🎯 Exception: Adenocard → push FAST! ⚡

🫁 Chest Tubes

Purpose: re-establish negative pressure in pleural space.

LocationRemovesMnemonic

Unilateral pneumohemothorax = 2 tubes (apical + basilar same side). Post-op chest surgery = same.

👶 CHDs, Infection Control & Crutches

Lec 3–4

❤️ Congenital Heart Defects

🔵 TRouBLe (R→L Shunt, Blue/Cyanotic)

  • All start with "T": Tetralogy of Fallot, Truncus, Transposition, Tricuspid stenosis, TAPVR
  • Exception: L ventricular hypoplastic syndrome
  • 🏥 Needs surgery, delayed growth

🩷 No TRouBLe (L→R Shunt, Pink)

  • VSD, PDA, PFO, ASD, pulmonic stenosis
  • Left to Right shunt, acyanotic
  • ✅ No surgery needed, normal growth
ALL CHD kids: murmurs + echocardiogram. Tetralogy = VarieD PictureS Of A RancH → VD, PS, OA, RH

🛡️ Transmission-Based Precautions

Droplet 💧Meningitis, H. influenza b (epiglottitis)Hand wash → mask → goggle → gloves. Patient wears mask outside room.

Functional PsychosisSchizophrenia, major depression, mania4 steps: Acknowledge feeling → Present reality → Set limit → Enforce

📖 Key Psych Terms

  • 💭 Delusion: false fixed belief (paranoid, grandiose, somatic). No sensory component.
  • 👁️ Hallucination: false fixed sensory experience (auditory, tactile, visual, gustatory, olfactory)
  • 🪞 Illusion: misinterpretation of reality — there IS a referent in reality
  • 🗣️ Loosening of association: flight of ideas (stringing phrases), word salad, neologisms
  • 👀 Ideas of reference: thinks everyone is talking about them
  • 🔒 Narrowed self-concept: psychotic refuses to change clothes/leave room — don't force them

🏃 Exercise & Sick Day Rules

  • 🏋️ Exercise potentiates insulin: more exercise → less insulin; less exercise → more insulin
  • 🤒 Sick days: take insulin, sip water, stay active. Blood sugar goes UP when sick.
  • 📊 Accu-check needed for: diabetes, TPN, steroids

⚠️ Acute Complications

🧊 Hypoglycemia (Insulin Shock)

  • Cause: not enough food, too much insulin/exercise
  • S/S: "Drunk in shock" — cold, clammy, staggering, slurred 🥶
  • Tx: fast sugar + protein (OJ & crackers 🧃)
  • Unconscious: Glucagon IM or D50 IV
  • 💀 Danger: permanent brain damage

🔥 DKA (Type 1) / HHNK (Type 2)

  • DKA: #1 cause = acute viral URI in last 10 days 🤧
  • DKA S/S: Dehydration, Ketones, Kussmaul, ↑K⁺, Acidosis, Acetone breath
  • Tx DKA: Regular Insulin IV, hydrate 200mL/hr 💧
  • HHNK: severe dehydration. Hot, dry, ↑HR. Tx: rehydrate
Cold & clammy 🧊 → get some candy. Hot & dry 🔥 → sugar's high. HbA1c = best indicator of long-term glucose control.

🦋 Endocrine

🔥 Hyperthyroidism (Graves')

  • = Hypermetabolism. Weight loss, diarrhea, ↑HR, hot, HTN, exophthalmos 👁️
  • Tx: radioactive iodine ☢️ (double flush, private bathroom), PTU (↓WBC), thyroidectomy
  • "Run yourself into the Grave" 🏃

🧊 Hypothyroidism (Myxedema)

  • = Hypometabolism. Weight gain, constipation, lethargy, cold, "slow" 🐢
  • Tx: thyroid replacement (Synthroid)
  • 🚨 Do NOT sedate. NEVER hold thyroid pills when NPO.

💊 Anticholinergic Pharmacology

Blocks acetylcholine → dries out and shuts down GI/GU motility. 🏜️

  • The ABCDs: Anorexia, Blurry vision, Constipation/Confusion, Dry Mouth, Sedation/Stasis of urine
  • The 4 D's: Dry Mouth, Dry Stool, Dilated Pupils (Mydriasis) 👁️, Dilated Bladder (retention)
  • Other Signs: Tachycardia 💓, anhidrosis (no sweat), absent bowel sounds
Atropine toxicity: "Hot as a Hare 🐰, Mad as a Hatter 🎩, Red as a Beet, Dry as a Bone 🦴, Blind as a Bat 🦇." Antidote: Physostigmine.

Priority 1: Urinary retention → increase fluids (2–3L/day), high fiber. Priority 2: Dry mouth → sugarless gum, hard candy 🍬, ice chips (do NOT hold drug).

🚨 NEVER give anticholinergics (Atropine, Tolterodine) to patients with Glaucoma → increases intraocular pressure.
🎯 Geodon prolongs QT → 💀 cardiac arrest (Black Box Warning).

⚖️ Anticholinergic vs. Cholinergic

SecretionsDry mouth, anhidrosisSalivation, lacrimation, diaphoresis

OphthalmicBlurry vision, dilated pupils 👁️Constricted pupils (miosis)
Overmedication with cholinergics (like Myasthenia Gravis drugs) → Cholinergic Crisis (SLUDGE signs) 💧🫁.

  • Stage 4: Recovery. 4 things, 4× per hour: vitals, fundus (boggy→massage; displaced→cath), pads (saturated ≤15 min = bad ⚠️), roll patient.

Lochia: Rubra 🔴 (first days) → Serosa 🩷 (week). Moderate = 4–6 inches on pad in 1 hr. Excessive = pad saturated in 15 min.

🚨 Prioritization, Delegation, Peds & Burns

Lec 12+

🎯 4 Prioritization Rules

  1. Acute beats chronic ⏱️
  2. Fresh post-op (<12 hrs) beats medical or other surgical 🏥
  3. Unstable beats stable — always! 🚨
  4. Vital organ tie-breaker: 🧠 Brain → 🫁 Lungs → ❤️ Heart → 🫘 Liver → 🫘 Kidney → Pancreas
Focus on the MODIFYING PHRASE, not the diagnosis!
🎯 4 patients are ALWAYS unstable: hemorrhage 🩸, hypoglycemia, fever ≥104 🌡️, pulseless/breathless.

🔴 Unstable Indicators

  • Acute illness, post-op <12 hrs ⏱️
  • General anesthesia, C/D labs
  • "Newly admitted/diagnosed" 🆕
  • Changed/changing assessment 📈

🟢 Stable Indicators

  • Chronic illness, post-op >12 hrs
  • Local/regional, A/B labs
  • "To be discharged" 🚪
  • Unchanged assessment
