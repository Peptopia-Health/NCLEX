🌸 Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs)

Peptopia Health • Clinical Mechanics Compendium

🚺 Section I: Clinical Overview & Nursing Priorities

Effective STI management requires rapid identification, strict adherence to pharmacological protocols, and comprehensive education to break the cycle of transmission.

🎯 Screening: Routine screening for ages 13-64 and high-risk pregnancy.

🎯 Eradication: Completing the full course even if symptoms resolve.

🎯 Prevention: Education on barrier methods and vaccine series completion.

🎯 Reporting: Legally mandated reporting of Chlamydia, Gonorrhea, and Syphilis.

💡 Section III: Critical Patient Education & Teaching

🚫 The "Seven Day Rule"

Abstain from all sexual activity (including oral and anal) for 7 days after completing a single dose (Azithromycin) or until the entire 7-day course of Doxycycline is completed. Clients remain infectious during this window.

🧴 Prevention Protocol

  • Douching is strictly contraindicated; it alters vaginal pH and pushes bacteria into the upper reproductive tract (increasing PID risk).
  • Latex condoms must be used consistently. OCPs and spermicides do not prevent STI transmission.
  • Partner treatment is mandatory; partners within the last 60 days should be treated via EPT.

🦠 Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID)

A severe ascending infection often caused by Chlamydia or Gonorrhea. Classic triad: Lower Abdominal Pain, Fever, and Cervical Motion Tenderness ("Chandelier Sign"). Risk of chronic pain and infertility.

🍄 Secondary Candidiasis

Broad-spectrum antibiotics like Doxycycline kill normal flora. Monitor for thick, white, "cottage cheese" discharge. Treat with Fluconazole (Diflucan).

🧪 Section IV: Diagnostics & Expected Interventions

NAAT Testing: The gold standard for Chlamydia/Gonorrhea. Uses urine or vaginal swabs. Highly sensitive.

Pregnancy: Universal Syphilis screening at 1st visit and 28 weeks. Treat with Penicillin G only (Desensitize if allergic).

Neonatal: Mandatory Erythromycin ophthalmic ointment for all newborns to prevent blindness from Gonorrhea.

Infection Control: Standard Precautions are sufficient. STIs are transmitted via mucosal contact, not casual interaction. Contact precautions are not expected.

🌸 MUST KNOW

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: Metronidazole (Flagyl) mixed with alcohol causes a severe disulfiram-like reaction (violent vomiting).

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: Secondary Syphilis is identified by a maculopapular rash on the palms of the hands and soles of the feet.

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: A Strawberry Cervix (petechiae) is the pathognomonic sign for Trichomoniasis.

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: Doxycycline is strictly contraindicated in pregnancy due to fetal tooth discoloration and bone growth inhibition.

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: HPV types 16 and 18 are highly oncogenic and responsible for the majority of cervical cancers.

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID) is the leading cause of ectopic pregnancy and tubal infertility in women.

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: Chlamydia is the most commonly reported STI and is frequently asymptomatic in females.

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: Expedited Partner Therapy (EPT) allows nurses to provide prescriptions for partners without a formal examination.

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: A painless Chancre (primary Syphilis) will disappear on its own, but the patient remains infectious.

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: Gonorrhea can disseminate (DGI), leading to Septic Arthritis and painful joint swelling.

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: Genital Herpes (HSV) shedding occurs even when no visible lesions are present; use barriers always.

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: Gardasil-9 is most effective when administered prior to the first sexual encounter (ages 11-12).

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: Hepatitis B is the only STI for which a vaccine can prevent eventual liver cancer (HCC).

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: Re-screening for Chlamydia is recommended 3 months after treatment to check for reinfection.

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: Scabies requires Permethrin 5% applied from the neck down for 8-14 hours.

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: Pubic Lice (Crabs) can be found in eyebrows and eyelashes; treat with Permethrin 1%.

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: Neurosyphilis can occur at any stage and requires high-dose intravenous Penicillin G.

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: Candidiasis (Yeast Infection) presents with a "cottage cheese" discharge after antibiotic use.

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: HIV screening is mandatory for any patient diagnosed with a new STI.

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: Congenital Syphilis is prevented by treating the mother with Penicillin at least 30 days before delivery.

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: Standard Precautions are the appropriate protocol; no special isolation is required for STI care.

🎯 NEED TO KNOW: The 7-day abstinence rule applies to the patient AND all partners simultaneously.
