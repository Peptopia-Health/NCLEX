Blood Pressure, Angina & Nitroglycerin

Cardiovascular

Blood Pressure Fundamentals

  • Systole = maximal force of blood on artery walls · Diastole = lowest force
  • Cessation of sounds = 2nd diastolic sound
  • Pulse Pressure = Systolic − Diastolic. Shock: narrows · ICP: widens

Too wide cuffFalse LOWDistributes pressure too broadly

Angina Pectoris

  • Chest pain from decreased blood supply to myocardium (ischemia)
  • Pain: crushing, substernal, radiating to left arm/jaw
  • 🧠 Key distinction: Angina IS relieved by rest/nitroglycerin. MI is NOT.

Nitroglycerin Protocol

ParameterDetails
RouteSublingual (under the tongue)
Dosing1 tab q5 min × 3 — if no relief, call MD
ActionDilates coronary arteries → ↑ O₂ supply · Also reduces preload
Side EffectsHeadache and Hypotension
StorageKeep dry/cool. Replace every 6 months.
⚠️ Nitro paste: nurse MUST wear gloves during application to avoid absorbing the drug and developing headache.

Myocardial Infarction

Cardiovascular

MI Definition & Presentation

  • 💀 Necrosis (death) of myocardial cells due to coronary artery blockage
  • Pain: crushing, heavy, squeezing. Radiates to left arm/jaw. Duration >30 minutes.
  • S/S: nausea, vomiting, diaphoresis, cold/clammy skin
  • Males more likely than females. 50% of deaths occur within 1 hour of onset.

MI Treatment — MONA

🧠 MONA: Morphine, Oxygen, Nitroglycerin, Aspirin. Aspirin prevents platelet aggregation (clots).
DrugPurposeKey Point
MorphinePain relief & reduces preloadFirst-line analgesic for MI pain
OxygenIncrease O₂ to ischemic tissueGive immediately
NitroglycerinDilates coronary arteriesDoes NOT relieve MI pain (unlike angina)
AspirinAnti-plateletPrevents clot extension. Also: Plavix

MI Complications

  • 🚨 Top 3 complications: Cardiogenic shock, Arrhythmias (PVCs most common), CHF
  • PVCs (Premature Ventricular Contractions) → treat with Lidocaine or Amiodarone
  • Cardiogenic Shock = pump failure. IV fluids do NOT help. Tx: vasopressors/inotropes.

MI Diagnostics & Activity

DiagnosticKey Finding
Cardiac EnzymesTroponin rises earliest (3 hrs). Also CPK, LDH, SGOT
EKGPermanent ST wave changes. Pathologic Q waves = necrosis.
Activity OrderBed rest with bedside commode. Resume sexual activity 6 weeks post-DC.

CHF & Cardiac Pharmacology

Cardiovascular

Left vs. Right Sided Heart Failure

Left-Sided Failure

  • Fluid backs up into LUNGS
  • Dyspnea, Rales/Crackles
  • Orthopnea, Frothy bloody sputum
  • Can lead to Pulmonary Edema

Right-Sided Failure

  • Fluid backs up into BODY
  • JVD, Dependent Edema
  • Ascites, Weight Gain
  • Hepatomegaly

CHF Compensation & Treatment

  • Compensation: Hypertrophy → Dilation → Tachycardia. When these fail = decompensation.
  • 3 Drug Groups: Diuretics + Vasodilators + Digitalis → Reduce preload/afterload, increase contractility
  • Best indicator of fluid retention: Daily Weight. Report gain of 3 lbs in one week.
  • TED Hose: Apply before getting out of bed. Remove daily to assess skin.
🚨 NEVER massage calves of CHF patient → risk of dislodging clot (embolism).

Digitalis (Digoxin)

ParameterDetails
Action↑ strength (positive inotropic) · ↓ rate (negative chronotropic)
Before GivingCheck apical pulse. Hold if pulse <60.
Toxicity Signs⚠️ Anorexia, N/V, Yellow Vision (halos), Arrhythmia
Toxicity RiskHypokalemia potentiates toxicity — monitor K⁺!
Therapeutic Level1.0 – 2.0 ng/mL

CHF Diuretics

Lasix (Furosemide): loop diuretic. Causes loss of Potassium (K⁺). Monitor K⁺ levels closely. Encourage potassium-rich foods (bananas, oranges).

Hypertension

  • Persistent BP > 140/90. Increased diastolic is most damaging.
  • Organs affected: Brain, Eyes, Heart, Kidneys
  • Risk factors: Race (Blacks), Obesity, Oral Contraceptives, Aging
  • Treatment: Diuretics, Weight loss, Na⁺ restriction, Stress reduction
  • ⚠️ #1 Side effect of antihypertensives: Orthostatic Hypotension

Aneurysms & CV Disease in Pregnancy

Cardiovascular

Aneurysms

  • Abnormal widening/weakening of artery wall. 💀 BIG danger: RUPTURE → Shock and death.
  • Thoracic: widening of aorta. Post-op: chest tube.
  • Abdominal: most common symptom = pulsating mass above umbilicus. Often silent.
Nursing PriorityDetails
Vital SignsMonitor pulse and BP. Pulse changes BEFORE BP if rupture occurs.
ActivityAbsolute Bedrest. Do NOT turn, cough, deep breathe until stable.
Rupture🚨 ↓ LOC, tachycardia, hypotension. Priority: Get to OR ASAP.

CV Disease in Pregnancy

  • CD ranks 4th in maternal death. Primary cause: Rheumatic Heart Disease.
  • Pregnancy requires 30-50% increased cardiac output.
  • Decompensation signs: Sudden dyspnea, JVD, Murmurs, Crackles
  • #1 Treatment: REST. Drugs: Diuretics, Heparin, Digoxin
  • Analgesics (even Morphine) OK during labor to ↓ anxiety/preload. C-section NOT mandatory.

Polycythemia Vera

Increase in RBCs, WBCs, Platelets → thick blood. Sign: ruddy (red) complexion, enlarged spleen. Treatment: Phlebotomy.

COPD, Emphysema & Asthma

Respiratory

COPD — Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease

  • 3 Components: Asthma, Bronchitis, Emphysema
  • Signs: SOB, Wheezing, Cough, Cyanosis
  • ABG: Hypoxemia, Hypercapnia, Respiratory Acidosis
  • Chest: Barrel Chest (increased AP diameter). Fingers: Clubbing.
🚨 COPD = Hypoxic Drive (low O₂ stimulates breathing). Max 2 LPM via nasal cannula. High flow O₂ can stop breathing!

Emphysema

FeatureDetails
AlveoliOver-enlarged and Under-ventilated → Air Trapping
BreathingPursed Lip Breathing. Rapid/Grunting. Pink Puffer or Cyanotic.
DietFrequent small meals. Fluids: 3 Liters/day (Force fluids).

Asthma

  • Spasms and Inflammation of bronchioles
  • Signs: Wheezing, SOB, Cough (at night)
  • 🧠 Meds: Bronchodilator FIRST, then Steroid (opens airways before delivering steroid)

Pneumonia, PE & Pulmonary Edema

Respiratory

Pneumonia

  • Infection of alveoli. Cause: bacteria, virus, aspiration
  • ABG: Respiratory Alkalosis (early — hyperventilation blows off CO₂)

Pulmonary Embolism (PE)

FeatureDetails
SourceDVT from legs (most common)
1st Sign🚨 Dyspnea + pleuritic pain + tachycardia
Risk FactorsBed rest, Oral Contraceptives, Atrial Fibrillation
TreatmentOxygen + Anticoagulants
🧠 Right Atrial Fib → causes PE. Left Atrial Fib → causes Stroke. Remember: Right=Lungs, Left=Brain.

Pulmonary Edema

  • Fluid in lungs from Left Ventricular Failure
  • Sputum: Bloody and Frothy
  • Treatment: Elevate HOB → O₂ → Lasix → Morphine → Aminophylline → Call MD

TB, RDS, Cystic Fibrosis & Epiglottitis

Respiratory

Tuberculosis (TB)

FeatureDetails
OrganismMycobacterium tuberculosis
SignsNight sweats, Hemoptysis, Weight loss
TestMantoux (PPD). Positive if >10mm induration
IsolationAirborne — Negative Pressure room, N95, 6 air exchanges/hr
Drugs (STRIPE)Streptomycin, Rifampin, Isoniazid, Pyrazinamide, Ethambutol
⚡ Rifampin → red/orange urine. Isoniazid → give B6 (Pyridoxine) to prevent peripheral neuritis. Avoid alcohol (hepatotoxicity). Non-contagious after 3 negative sputum cultures.

RDS (Respiratory Distress Syndrome)

  • Occurs in premature infants. Cause: lack of surfactant.
  • Signs: Retractions, Nasal flaring, Grunting. 💀 Death within 96 hours if untreated.
  • L/S Ratio 2:1 = mature lungs
  • Treatment: Survanta via ET tube + PEEP/CPAP
⚠️ High O₂ = Retrolental Fibroplasia (blindness). High ventilator pressures = Bronchopulmonary Dysplasia.

Cystic Fibrosis (CF)

FeatureDetails
PathologyExocrine gland disorder → Thick mucous. Affects Lungs & Pancreas.
DiagnosisSweat Test (Iontophoresis) — >60 mEq/L Chloride
StoolsSteatorrhea: Fat, Frothy, Foul-smelling, Floating (4 F's)
DietHigh Calorie, High Protein. Pancreatic Enzymes with meals.
InterventionsPostural drainage (most common). Hot weather: NaCl tablets.

Epiglottitis

  • Inflammation of epiglottis — caused by H. influenza B
  • 🚨 #1 Danger: Airway Obstruction
  • Signs: Muffled voice, Drooling, Stridor, Tripod position
  • NEVER put a tongue blade in the mouth
  • Treatment: Antibiotics (Penicillin/Ampicillin). Prevention: Hib Vaccine.

ICP, Meningitis & Seizures

Neurological

Increased Intracranial Pressure (ICP)

FeatureDetails
Normal ICP0-15 mmHg
Earliest SignChange in LOC
Infant SignsBulging fontanels, High-pitched cry
Adult SignsHeadache, Projectile vomiting
Cushing's Triad (LATE)🚨 1. HTN with widening pulse pressure · 2. Bradycardia · 3. Irregular respirations
PositionHOB elevated 30°
MedsMannitol (osmotic diuretic) · Decadron (steroids to ↓ edema)

Meningitis

  • Inflammation of arachnoid/pia mater. Signs: Nuchal rigidity, photophobia, fever
  • Kernig's: straighten knee → leg pain. Brudzinski's: flex neck → knees flex.
  • Diagnosis: Lumbar Puncture (Cloudy CSF, Low Glucose)
  • Isolation: Contact + Respiratory precautions until on antibiotics for 48 hours
  • Opisthotonos (severe arching of back) → place in Side-lying position
⚡ Spinal Tap (LP): Needle at L3-L4. Post-op: Lie flat 6-12 hrs (prevent headache). Force fluids.

Seizures

  • Uncontrolled electrical discharge in the brain
  • ⚠️ Safety: Do NOT restrain. Do NOT put anything in mouth.
  • Medication: Dilantin (Phenytoin). Side effect: Gingival hyperplasia (gum overgrowth)

CVA, Spinal Cord & Laminectomy

Neurological

CVA (Stroke)

FeatureDetails
CausesThrombus, Embolus, Hemorrhage. #1 Risk: HTN
TIAWarning sign: Transient Ischemic Attack. Lasts 30 sec to 24 hrs.
First SignChange in LOC. Position: HOB up 30°. Fluid restricted.
HemiplegiaMotor tracts cross at medulla. Right brain → Left body paralysis.
HemianopsiaCan't see one half of visual field. Teach to SCAN (turn head).
AphasiaExpressive (Broca's) = can't speak. Receptive (Wernicke's) = can't understand. Global = both.

Spinal Cord Injury

  • #1 Goal: Immobilization
  • C1-C8 = Quadriplegic · Thoracic/Lumbar = Paraplegic
  • Spinal Shock: cord swells above and below injury. Duration: 5 days to 3 months.

Autonomic Dysreflexia

🚨 EMERGENCY! Occurs in injuries T6 or higher. Signs: Severe HTN, Headache, Bradycardia, Sweating. Trigger: Full bladder/bowel impaction.
  1. 1st Action: Elevate HOB (lowers BP)
  2. 2nd: Empty bladder (straight cath) then check bowel

Laminectomy

LocationPre-op AssessmentPost-op Complication
CervicalUpper extremities & breathingPneumonia
ThoracicCough & bowel soundsParalytic ileus
LumbarUrine output & legsUrinary retention

Post-op: LOG ROLL always. No dangling legs. No sitting >30 min. Restrictions for 6 weeks: no driving, no lifting >5 lbs.

Degenerative & Sensory Disorders

Neurological

Myasthenia Gravis

  • Defect in Acetylcholine at neuromuscular junction. Weakness worsens with activity.
  • Diagnosis: Tensilon Test (positive = improvement in strength)
  • Medications: Anticholinesterases (-stigmine). Must be given EXACTLY on time (with food).
  • 💀 Crisis: Respiratory failure

Multiple Sclerosis

  • Demyelination of CNS. Pattern: Remissions & Exacerbations.
  • 🧠 Charcot's Triad: Tremor, Scanning speech, Nystagmus
  • Treatment: ACTH/Prednisone for acute exacerbations. Spasms: Baclofen/Dantrium
  • Urinary retention: Urecholine (Bethanechol)

Parkinson's Disease

  • Dopamine deficiency in Basal Ganglia
  • Signs: Tremor (pill-rolling), Rigidity (Cogwheel), Bradykinesia
  • Treatment: Levodopa/Carbidopa (Sinemet). Action: increases Dopamine. ⚠️ Avoid Vitamin B6.

Head Injury & GCS

TypeKey Difference
Epidural HematomaArterial (fast). Middle meningeal artery. Lucid interval.
Subdural HematomaVenous (slow). Bridging veins. Gradual deterioration.

Glasgow Coma Scale: Eye + Verbal + Motor. Max = 15. Coma = <8.

Renal, GI & Surgical Disorders

GI & Renal

Acute Glomerulonephritis (AGN)

  • Inflammation of glomeruli. Cause: Group A Beta-Hemolytic Strep
  • Signs: Puffiness of face, dark urine, Hematuria
  • Most important intervention: BEDREST. Diet: Moderate Na⁺ restriction.

💀 BIGGEST DANGER in renal failure: Hyperkalemia and its effect on the heart (peaked T waves, cardiac arrest).

Dialysis

TypeKey Points
HemodialysisAV Shunt/Fistula/Graft. NO BP or venipuncture in that arm. Feel for thrill, auscultate bruit.
PeritonealDialysate infused by gravity. Should come out straw-colored. 🚨 Cloudy = Peritonitis!

Disequilibrium Syndrome (hemodialysis): change in LOC, N/V, headache, twitching — from too rapid fluid/electrolyte shifts.
