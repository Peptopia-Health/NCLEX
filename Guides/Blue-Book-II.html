1 Acid-Base Balance

pH Fundamentals

ParameterNormal RangeInterpretation
pH7.35 – 7.45<7.35 = Acidosis · >7.45 = Alkalosis
PaCO₂35 – 45 mmHgRespiratory component. CO₂ = Acidosis
HCO₃⁻22 – 26 mEq/LMetabolic component. Bicarb = Acidosis
PaO₂80 – 100 mmHg<60 = Respiratory failure
ROME: Respiratory = Opposite (pH↓ CO₂↑), Metabolic = Equal (pH↓ HCO₃↓)

The Four Imbalances

ImbalancepHPrimary ChangeClassic Causes
Respiratory Acidosis↓CO₂ (retained)COPD, Overdose, Hypoventilation
Respiratory Alkalosis↑CO₂ (blown off)Hyperventilation, Anxiety, PE (early)
Metabolic Acidosis↓HCO₃⁻DKA, Renal failure, Shock, Diarrhea
Metabolic Alkalosis↑HCO₃⁻Vomiting, NG suction, Antacid overuse

Compensation

  • Lungs compensate for metabolic problems → adjust breathing rate (fast = blow off CO₂; slow = retain CO₂)
  • Kidneys compensate for respiratory problems → retain or excrete HCO₃⁻ (takes hours to days)
  • Kussmaul breathing: Deep rapid respirations → body trying to blow off CO₂ in metabolic acidosis (especially DKA)
Acidosis → Hyperkalemia (H⁺ moves INTO cells, K⁺ moves OUT). Alkalosis → Hypokalemia (H⁺ moves OUT, K⁺ moves IN). Both can cause cardiac arrest — always check K⁺ with any acid-base imbalance!
ABG Interpretation Steps: 1) Look at pH → acidosis or alkalosis? 2) Look at CO₂ → does it match? (respiratory). 3) Look at HCO₃ → does it match? (metabolic). 4) Both abnormal → compensation is occurring.
2 Alcohol & Substances

Alcoholism

  • Psychodynamic: Low self-esteem, dependency, difficulty expressing feelings
  • Defense mechanism: DENIAL (primary). Also projection, rationalization.
  • Wernicke's Encephalopathy: Confusion, ataxia, nystagmus → Give Thiamine (B1) BEFORE glucose!
  • Korsakoff's Syndrome: Permanent memory loss, confabulation (makes up stories). Irreversible.
Always give Thiamine (B1) BEFORE glucose in suspected alcohol withdrawal. Giving glucose first can precipitate Wernicke's encephalopathy.

Alcohol Withdrawal Timeline

Time After Last DrinkSymptoms
6-12 hoursTremors, anxiety, N/V, diaphoresis, tachycardia
12-24 hoursHallucinations (auditory/visual)
24-48 hoursSeizures (Grand Mal)
48-96 hoursDelirium Tremens (DTs) — medical emergency! Confusion, hallucinations, severe agitation, tachycardia, fever. Can be FATAL.

Treatment: Benzodiazepines (Librium, Ativan) for withdrawal. Disulfiram (Antabuse) for maintenance — causes severe N/V if patient drinks.

Key Rule: Withdrawal is the OPPOSITE of the drug's effect. Depressant withdrawal = excitation (tremors, seizures). Stimulant withdrawal = depression (fatigue, sleepiness).

3 Cardiac Meds, Aminoglycosides & Bioterrorism

Aminoglycosides

⚠️ Aminoglycoside Toxicity

OtotoxicityNephrotoxicityPeak & Trough
  • Drugs ending in -mycin / -micin: Gentamicin, Tobramycin, Amikacin, Streptomycin
  • Ototoxicity: hearing loss, tinnitus. Nephrotoxicity: ↑ BUN/Creatinine
  • Trough: drawn 30 min before dose. Peak: drawn 30 min after dose.
  • Assess hearing and renal function before and during therapy
Never give aminoglycosides concurrently with other oto/nephrotoxic drugs (e.g., Lasix, Vancomycin) without close monitoring.

Chest Tubes

  • Purpose: Restore negative pressure in pleural space
  • Placement: Air = upper (2nd intercostal). Fluid = lower (5th-6th).
  • Tidaling in water seal chamber is NORMAL
  • Continuous bubbling in water seal = air leak (NOT normal)
  • Keep petroleum jelly gauze at bedside for accidental dislodgement
Tube disconnects from system: Submerge end in sterile water. Tube falls OUT of patient: Cover with petroleum gauze taped on 3 sides.

CCBs & Bioterrorism

💊 Calcium Channel Blockers

  • Verapamil, Diltiazem, Nifedipine, Amlodipine
  • ↓ BP, ↓ HR, ↓ contractility
  • No Grapefruit juice

☣️ Bioterrorism Agents

  • Anthrax: Black eschar, NOT contagious
  • Smallpox: Vesicular rash, Airborne
  • Botulism: Descending paralysis
  • Ricin: No antidote

🦯 Crutch Fitting & Safety

FittingNerve Risk
  • 2-3 finger widths between axilla and crutch pad
  • Weight bears on HANDS, NOT axillae
  • Numbness/tingling in hands = crutch palsy (nerve compression)
"Up with the Good, Down with the Bad" — Going UP stairs: good leg goes first. Going DOWN stairs: bad (affected) leg + crutches go first.

Assistive Devices

  • Cane: Hold on the STRONG (unaffected) side. Advance cane with the weak leg.
  • Walker: Advance walker → weak leg → strong leg. All 4 legs must touch ground before stepping.

5 Diabetes Mellitus
Type 1 (IDDM)
VS
Type 2 (NIDDM)

🔴 Type 1

  • NO insulin production
  • Onset: childhood/young adult
  • Thin body habitus
  • Requires insulin injections
  • Prone to DKA

🔵 Type 2

  • Insulin resistance
  • Onset: adult (usually >40)
  • Often obese
  • Oral hypoglycemics → may need insulin
  • Prone to HHS (HHNS)

Insulin Types

Hypoglycemia occurs at PEAK time — know your insulin peaks! Clear before Cloudy when mixing: draw Regular (clear) before NPH (cloudy). Only Regular insulin can be given IV.

9 Psych Medications

SSRIs

  • Drugs: Fluoxetine (Prozac), Sertraline (Zoloft), Paroxetine (Paxil), Escitalopram (Lexapro)
  • Takes 2-4 weeks for therapeutic effect — educate patient to keep taking!
  • Side effects: Sexual dysfunction, GI upset, insomnia
Serotonin Syndrome (combining serotonergic drugs): Fever, agitation, tremors, diarrhea, muscle rigidity, hyperreflexia → medical emergency!

MAOIs

🚨 MAOI Dietary Restriction

Hypertensive CrisisTyramine
  • Drugs: Phenelzine (Nardil), Tranylcypromine (Parnate)
  • Avoid TYRAMINE: Aged cheese, Wine, Beer, Smoked meats, Soy sauce, Pickled/fermented foods
  • Danger: Hypertensive Crisis (severe HTN, headache, neck stiffness)
  • Do NOT combine with SSRIs, Meperidine, or sympathomimetics
SSRI to MAOI switch: Must have a 2-week washout period (5 weeks for Fluoxetine due to long half-life) to prevent Serotonin Syndrome.

Typical Antipsychotics

  • Haloperidol (Haldol), Chlorpromazine (Thorazine)
  • EPS: Dystonia, Akathisia, Tardive Dyskinesia (irreversible lip smacking!), Parkinsonism
  • EPS treatment: Cogentin or Benadryl
Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome (NMS): Fever >104°F, "lead pipe" rigidity, altered LOC, autonomic instability. STOP drug immediately! Treat with Dantrolene + cooling.

Atypical Antipsychotics & Mood Stabilizers

🧪 Atypical Antipsychotics

  • Clozapine, Risperidone, Olanzapine, Quetiapine
  • Less EPS, but Metabolic Syndrome
  • Clozapine: Agranulocytosis — monitor WBC weekly!

⚖️ Lithium

  • Therapeutic: 0.6-1.2 mEq/L
  • Toxicity: Tremors → N/V → Ataxia → Seizures → Coma
  • Maintain Na⁺ and fluids — dehydration = ↑ lithium
  • Draw levels 12 hours after last dose
10 Maternity & OB

Pregnancy Basics

  • Nagele's Rule: LMP + 7 days − 3 months = Estimated Due Date
  • Presumptive: Amenorrhea, N/V, Breast tenderness, Fatigue, Quickening
  • Probable: Positive hCG, Chadwick's (blue cervix), Goodell's (soft cervix), Hegar's (soft uterus)
  • Positive: FHR by Doppler, Ultrasound visualization, Fetal movement by examiner

Postpartum & Preeclampsia

🚨 Preeclampsia

HTN >140/90ProteinuriaAfter 20 Weeks
  • Triad: HTN + Proteinuria + Edema (face/hands)
  • Severe: HTN >160/110, headache, visual changes, epigastric pain, HELLP
  • Treatment: Magnesium Sulfate (seizure prevention). Antidote: Calcium Gluconate
  • Eclampsia = preeclampsia + SEIZURES
Mag Sulfate toxicity: ↓ DTRs (check reflexes!), ↓ respirations (<12/min = HOLD), ↓ urine output (<30 mL/hr). Keep Calcium Gluconate at bedside. Boggy uterus = #1 cause postpartum hemorrhage → massage the fundus!

12 Prioritization & Delegation

Triage — ABCs & Maslow

  1. Airway → first priority always
  2. Breathing → ensure adequate ventilation
  3. Circulation → assess perfusion, bleeding
  4. Safety → environmental and patient safety
  5. Psychosocial → emotional and social needs last
Acute over Chronic. Unstable over Stable. New onset over Established. Unexpected over Expected. Use ABCs when all options are physiological. Use Maslow when options span different levels.

Five Rights of Delegation

  1. Right Task — Can this be delegated? (Routine, non-complex)
  2. Right Circumstance — Is the patient stable enough?
  3. Right Person — Is the delegate competent & within scope?
  4. Right Direction — Clear, concise instructions given?
  5. Right Supervision — RN follows up and evaluates outcomes
The RN can NEVER delegate: Assessment, Teaching, Evaluation, or anything requiring nursing judgment. The RN ALWAYS retains accountability.

Key Psych Disorders — Quick Review

😞 Depression

  • Suicide risk highest when improving (has energy to act)
  • Ask directly about suicidal ideation

🎢 Mania

  • Safety first. High-cal finger foods
  • Decrease stimuli, firm limits

😰 Panic

  • Stay with patient. Short sentences.
  • Breathing techniques. Do NOT leave.

🔁 OCD

  • Do NOT interrupt rituals initially
  • Gradually limit time for rituals

🍽️ Anorexia

  • Monitor refeeding syndrome
  • Sit with pt 30-60 min after meals

🧩 Schizophrenia

  • Don't argue with hallucinations
  • Present reality, focus on feelings

NCLEX Test-Taking Strategies

  • Assessment before Intervention — unless life-threatening emergency → intervene first
  • Safety first — when in doubt, choose the safest answer
  • Therapeutic communication → acknowledge feelings, open-ended, don't give advice
  • Never "Call the doctor" first unless you've exhausted nursing interventions
  • Avoid absolutes: "always," "never," "all," "none" are usually WRONG
  • "Which patient first?" → Most acute, unstable, or unexpected finding
SATA (Select All That Apply): Treat each option independently as true/false. Don't look for a pattern. Each option stands on its own merits.
