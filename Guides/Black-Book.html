Acid-Base Balance & Ventilators
Lecture 1 · pH interpretation, compensations & ventilator alarms
Normal Values
pH

7.35 – 7.45
<7.35 = Acidosis
>7.45 = Alkalosis

CO₂

35 – 45

HCO₃ (Bicarb)

22 – 26

Rule of the B's: If pH and Bicarb move Both in the same direction → Metabolic. Otherwise → Respiratory.
Worked Examples
ExamplepHHCO₃DirectionDiagnosis
#17.30 ↓20 ↓SameMetabolic Acidosis
#27.58 ↑32 ↑SameMetabolic Alkalosis

"As the pH goes, so goes my patient — EXCEPT for Potassium."
• pH ↓ (acidosis) → everything ↓ except K⁺ (hyperkalemia)
• pH ↑ (alkalosis) → everything ↑ except K⁺ (hypokalemia)

Identifying the Cause

Decision Tree

  1. Is it LUNG? → Respiratory
    • Underventilating → Respiratory Acidosis
    • Overventilating → Respiratory Alkalosis
  2. Not lung? → Metabolic
    • Prolonged gastric vomiting/suction → Metabolic Alkalosis
    • Everything else → Metabolic Acidosis (default!)
Default setting: When you don't know what to pick → pick Metabolic Acidosis.
Always pay attention to the modifying phrase rather than the original noun.
Ventilators
HIGH PRESSURE ALARM vs LOW PRESSURE ALARM
High Pressure AlarmLow Pressure Alarm
Increased resistance to airflowDecreased resistance
Causes: Kinks, water in tubing, mucus plugsCauses: Main tubing or O₂ sensor disconnection
High Pressure Alarm — Order of Actions
  1. Unkink the tubing
  2. Empty water out of tubing
  3. Ask pt to turn, cough, deep breathe
  4. Suction PRN

Ventilator Settings

• Too high → Patient overventilated → Respiratory Alkalosis (panting)
• Too low → Patient underventilated → Respiratory Acidosis (retaining CO₂)

Weaning question: If ABGs show respiratory acidosis → Notify physician, pt is NOT ready to wean (underventilated). If respiratory alkalosis → Ready to wean (overventilating).

Alcohol & Substances
Lecture 2 · Denial, OD/WD, Aminoglycosides, Peak & Trough
Alcoholism — Psychological Problems
#1 Denial

Confront by pointing out difference between what they say and do. Use "I" statements (not "YOU"). Exception: In loss & grief, denial is OK (DABDA).

#2 Dependency / Co-Dependency

Dependent (abuser) → Confront
Co-dependent (SO) → Teach to set limits, work on self-esteem

#3 Manipulation

When SO is asked to do something inherently dangerous/harmful. Treatment: Set limits and enforce.

Dependency vs Manipulation: If the act is NOT inherently dangerous = Dependency/Co-dependency (2 patients). If the act IS dangerous = Manipulation (1 patient).
Wernicke-Korsakoff Syndrome

Wernicke-Korsakoff

PsychosisVitamin B1 Deficiency

• Primary S/Sx: Amnesia + Confabulation (making up stories that feel real to pt)
• Intervention: Redirect to something they can do
• Characteristics: Preventable (take B1), Arrestable (take B1), Irreversible 70% (kills brain cells)

Antabuse & Revia (Disulfiram)

Antabuse/Revia

• Aversion therapy: onset & duration = 2 weeks
• Teach to avoid ALL forms of EtOH: mouthwash, cologne, perfume, aftershave, elixir, OTC liquid meds, insect repellant, hand sanitizer, vanilla extract
• Red wine vinaigrette is OK — no alcohol in it

Aminoglycosides — "A Mean Old Mysin"
"A Mean Old Mysin" = Aminoglycosides. Use a mean old mycin for mean old infections (serious, resistant, life-threatening, Gram-negative).

Calcium Channel Blockers

CCBs — "Valium for the Heart"

• Negative inotropic, chronotropic, dromotropic → relax & slow the heart
• Treat "A, AA, AAA": Anti-HTN, AntiAnginal, AntiAtrialArrhythmia

Chest Tubes

Purpose

Reestablish negative pressure in pleural space so lungs expand with chest wall.

Apical = Air (top). Basilar = Blood (base, gravity).

Water Seal Breaks — Action Steps
  1. Clamp the tube
  2. Cut the tube away
  3. Submerge end under sterile water (most important — reestablishes seal)
  4. Unclamp the tube
Must complete in ≤15 seconds! "First" action = clamp. "Best/Priority" action = submerge under sterile water.
Chest tube pulled out: (1) Gloved hand over opening (first). (2) Sterile Vaseline gauze taped on 3 sides (best).

Congenital Heart Defects
"TRouBLe" — T = Trouble. Shunts Right to Left. Blue (cyanotic). All start with "T":
Tetralogy of Fallot · Truncus arteriosus · Transposition of great vessels · Tricuspid atresia · TAPV
Exception: Left ventricular hypoplastic syndrome
Tetralogy of Fallot = "PROVe": Pulmonary artery stenosis, RVH, Overriding aorta, VSD.
No TRouBLe: VSD, PDA, Patent foramen ovale, ASD, Pulmonic stenosis.
ALL congenital defects have a murmur → order echocardiogram.

Crutch Measurement & Gaits

Crutch Fitting

• 2–3 finger widths between pad and anterior axillary fold
• Tip: 6 inches lateral and 6 inches in front of foot
• Elbow flexion: 30°. Wrists at level of handgrip.

GaitPatternUse When
2-Point(R leg + L crutch) → (L leg + R crutch)Mild bilateral weakness
3-Point(2 crutches + bad leg) → good legOne leg affected
4-PointCrutch → opposite foot → other crutch → other footSevere bilateral weakness
Swing-ThroughLike 3-point but foot passes crutch tipsNon-weight bearing / amputees
Even-point for even (bilateral) problems. Odd-point for odd (one leg).
Mild bilateral = 2-pt. Severe bilateral = 4-pt. One leg = 3-pt. Amputee = Swing-through.
"Up with the Good, Down with the Bad." Both crutches always move with the bad leg.
Cane: Hold on the strong/unaffected side. Walker: Pick up → Set down → Walk to it.

Acute Complications

Hypoglycemia — "Drunk in Shock"

Low Blood Sugar#1 cause: Too much insulin

Drunk: Staggering gait, slurred speech, cerebral impairment, slow reactions
Shock: Tachycardia, tachypnea, low BP, cold/clammy/mottled

Treatment: Sugar + starch/protein (juice + turkey, skim milk). Bad: 2 sugars together.
Unconscious at home → Glucagon IM. In ER → Dextrose IV (D10, D50)

DKA — Type 1 High Glucose

EmergencyKetosis

"DKA" = Dehydration (dry, warm) · Ketones + Kussmaul + K⁺↑ · Acidosis + Acetone breath + Anorexia
#1 cause: Acute viral URI within last 2 weeks
Treatment: Regular insulin IV + IV fluid 200 mL/hr

HHNK/HHS — Type 2 High Glucose

Higher mortality than DKA

No ketones, no acid. Think SEVERE dehydration.
#1 Dx: Fluid volume deficit. #1 Intervention: Rehydrate!

HbA1C: Average blood sugar over 90 days. <6 = normal. 7 = borderline. >8 = out of control.
Long-term complications: Poor tissue perfusion (renal failure, gangrene) OR peripheral neuropathy (incontinence, can't feel burns).

Electrolytes
3 Rules to Memorize:
1. Kalemias go SAME direction as prefix, EXCEPT HR & urine output (opposite)
2. Calcemias go OPPOSITE direction as prefix (calcium is a sedative)
3. Magnesemias go OPPOSITE direction as prefix (also a sedative)
ElectrolyteHyper-Hypo-
K⁺ (Kalemia)Seizures, agitation, diarrhea, spasticity, 3+/4+ reflexes, bradycardia, oliguriaLethargy, constipation, flaccid, 0/1+ reflexes, tachycardia, polyuria
Ca²⁺ (Calcemia)Bradycardia, lethargy, flaccid, constipation (things DOWN)Agitation, seizures, spasms, Chvostek, Trousseau (things UP)
Na⁺ (Natremia)DEhydration (hot, flushed, dry, thready pulse) — "E" = DEOverload (crackles, distended neck veins) — "O" = Overload
K⁺ for heart problems. Ca²⁺ for muscle/nerve problems.
Earliest sign of ANY electrolyte disturbance = Paresthesia (numbness/tingling).
Universal sign = Paresis (muscle weakness).
Treatment of Hyperkalemia
High K⁺ can stop the heart!
  1. D5W + Regular Insulin — drives K⁺ into cells (fast but temporary)
  2. Kayexalate — exchanges K⁺ for Na⁺ via enema/PO (takes hours — "K Exits Late")
  3. Give both together for immediate + sustained effect
Never push potassium IV. K⁺ <40 mEq/L of IV fluid. If >40 → clarify with physician.

Psychiatric Drugs
Lecture 9 · Phenothiazines, TCAs, benzos, MAOIs, lithium, SSRIs, atypicals
ALL psych drugs cause: Low BP + Weight changes (usually gain).
Naming: "ZINEs for the ZANY" (major antipsychotics). "ZEPs for ZZZs" (minor/anti-anxiety). "ZAPINEs" = 2nd gen antipsychotics.
Phenothiazines (1st Gen Antipsychotics)

Phenothiazines — Major Tranquilizers

End in "ZINE"Thorazine, Compazine

S/E = "ABCDEFG":
Anticholinergic (dry mouth, urinary retention) · Blurred vision · Constipation · Drowsiness · EPS (tremors, Parkinson) · Foto sensitivity · aGranulocytosis (low WBC → teach to report sore throat/infection)

S/E → Educate. Toxic effect → HOLD drug + Notify HCP.
#1 Dx for A–F: Safety. #1 Dx for G: Report infections.

Tricyclic Antidepressants (TCAs)

TCAs — Mood Elevators

Elavil, Tofranil, Aventyl, Desyrel

S/E = "ABCDE" (same as above + Euphoria). Takes 2–4 weeks for therapeutic effect. Teach patience.

Benzodiazepines (Anti-Anxiety)

Benzos — Minor Tranquilizers

Have "ZEP" in namePam or Lam endings

Prototype: Valium. Uses: anti-anxiety, seizures, pre-op induction, muscle relaxant, EtOH withdrawal, vent sedation.
Work quickly but don't use >2–4 weeks. S/E = "ABCD".

Give major + minor tranquilizers together (e.g., Valium + Elavil). Minor works immediately; major takes 2-4 weeks. Discontinue minor once major kicks in. Same principle as Heparin + Coumadin.
MAO Inhibitors

MAOIs — Antidepressants

MARplan, NARdil, PARnateTyramine = Hypertensive Crisis

S/E: ABCD. AVOID tyramine-containing foods:
Fruits: "BAR" — Bananas, Avocados, Raisins · Grains OK except yeast · Meats: No organs, no preserved (smoked, dried, cured, pickled, hot dogs) · Dairy: No aged cheese (mozzarella & cottage OK) · No EtOH, caffeine, chocolate, licorice, soy sauce

Lithium

Lithium — Bipolar (LI = BI)

Acts like an electrolyteTherapeutic: 0.6–1.2Toxic: >2.0

S/E (3 Ps): Peeing (polyuria), Pooping (diarrhea), Paresthesia
Toxic: Tremors, metallic taste, severe diarrhea
#1 Intervention (both S/E & toxic): Give fluids. Low Na⁺ → lithium toxic. High Na⁺ → lithium ineffective. Keep Na⁺ normal.

Prozac, Haldol, Clozaril, Zoloft
Prozac (SSRI)

S/E: ABCDE. Causes insomnia → give before noon. Watch for suicidal risk in adolescents with dose changes.

Haldol (1st Gen)

S/E: ABCDEFG (same as phenothiazines). NMS risk (high fever >105). Differentiate NMS vs EPS: check temperature.

Clozaril (Atypical)

Advantage: No A-F side effects. Danger: Agranulocytosis (worse than chemo!). #1 intervention: Monitor WBC.

Zoloft (SSRI)

Can cause insomnia. Interacts with St. John's Wort → Serotonin syndrome "SAD Head": Sweating, Apprehensive, Dizzy, Headache. Interacts with Warfarin → bleeding.

Geodon (ziprasidone): Black box warning — prolongs QT interval → sudden cardiac arrest. Do NOT use in cardiac patients.

Pregnancy Basics
Naegele Rule: First day of LMP + 7 days − 3 months = EDD.
Example: LMP June 10 → June 17 → March 17.
Weight Gain

Ideal Weight Gain

1st trimester: 1 lb/month (3 lbs total). 2nd & 3rd: 1 lb/week. Ideal total: 25–31 lbs.
Quick formula: Weeks gestation − 9 = ideal lbs gained.
±1-2 lbs = WNL. ±3 = Assess. ±4+ = Trouble (BPP on fetus).

Lecture 12 · 4 rules, staff management, pediatric teaching, Piaget, guessing strategies
This is the most important lecture — at least 15 questions on the exam. Do not guess in this section — learn the material!
4 Rules for Prioritization

Rule 1: Acute Beats Chronic

Acute appendicitis > COPD or CHF. An acutely ill patient always takes priority.

Rule 2: Fresh Post-op (<12 hrs) Beats Medical/Other Surgical

2-hr postcholecystectomy > radical neck dissection > bilateral AKA > craniotomy. Fresh post-op always wins.

Rule 3: Unstable Beats Stable

Answers have 4 parts: (1) Age, (2) Gender, (3) Dx, (4) Modifying phrase. The modifying phrase is ALWAYS most important. Age & gender are irrelevant for prioritization.

STABLE vs UNSTABLE
StableUnstable
Stable, chronic, >12hr post-opUnstable, acute, <12hr post-op
Local/regional anesthesiaGeneral anesthesia in first 12 hrs
Labs A or B levelLabs C or D level
Ready for discharge, >24hr admittedNewly diagnosed/admitted, <24hr
Unchanged assessmentChanging/changed assessment
Expected S/Sx of diseaseUnexpected S/Sx
4 Things That ALWAYS Make You Unstable (even if expected):
1. Hemorrhage (not just bleeding) · 2. High fever >105°F · 3. Hypoglycemia · 4. Pulseless or Breathless
Exception: At unwitnessed accident scene, pulseless/breathless = low priority (likely dead) = BLACK TAG

Rule 4: Tie-Breaker — Organ Vitality

Use the modifying phrase organ (not diagnosis organ):
1. Brain → 2. Lung → 3. Heart → 4. Liver → 5. Kidney → 6. Pancreas

Mass Casualty BLACK TAG: Pulseless, Breathless, or Fixed/dilated pupils → "Tag them black, ship them last."

7 Psych Test Principles
  1. Know what phase of the nurse-patient relationship you're in
  2. Don't give/accept gifts in psych
  3. Don't give advice! Ask "What do YOU think you should do?"
  4. Never give guarantees ("If you cry, you'll feel better")
  5. Immediacy: Pick the answer that keeps them talking
  6. Concreteness: No slang, no figurative speech. Psych pts take you literally.
  7. Empathy: Accept feelings. Never say "Don't worry" or "I know how you feel."
Guessing Strategies
#Strategy
1Psych: Pick "I will examine my feelings" or "Establish trust relationship"
2Nutrition: Pick "baked chicken" (no fried). No chicken? Pick fish (not shellfish). Toddlers: finger food. Don't mix meds in kids' food.
3Pharm: If unknown S/E, pick S/E in same body system as drug. Unknown PO drug → pick GI S/E.
4OB: Check fetal heart rate
5Med-Surg: "First thing to assess?" = LOC. "First thing to do?" = Establish airway.
6Peds: "NORMAL, OLDER, EASIER" — Give the child more time
7General: Rule out absolutes. Same-meaning answers are both wrong. Opposites → one is likely right.
8Priority: Pick the worst consequence (Worst Consequences Game)
9Stuck between 2? Re-read the question — you missed something
10Sesame Street Rule: The answer that looks different from the others is likely correct
